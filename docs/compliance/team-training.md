# Manual Fallback, Fraud Review, and ML Oversight Training Plan

This program aligns support, compliance, and regulator-relations teams on their
shared responsibilities when automated controls are degraded or escalated.

## Training Cadence
- **Onboarding:** Mandatory within 30 days of hire; includes LMS modules and
  hands-on sandbox drills.
- **Quarterly refreshers:** 90-minute instructor-led sessions covering updates
  to fallback runbooks, fraud typologies, and oversight procedures.
- **Tabletop exercises:** Semi-annual cross-team simulations validating manual
  fallback readiness and regulator communications.

## Curriculum Overview
| Module | Audience | Delivery | Key Outcomes |
| ------ | -------- | -------- | ------------ |
| Manual Fallback Playbooks | Support & Compliance | Live workshop with runbook walkthroughs | Trigger, execute, and document fallback activation with accurate customer comms. |
| Fraud-Review Workflows | Support & Compliance | Case-study labs using synthetic fraud alerts | Escalate to analysts, capture evidence packages, and record regulator notifications. |
| ML Oversight Responsibilities | Compliance & Regulator Relations | Guided review of model cards and monitoring dashboards | Interpret fairness/drift dashboards, schedule audits, and brief regulators. |
| Regulator Communications | Regulator Relations & Compliance | Scenario-based role play | Deliver clear status updates referencing `status/roadmap.md` evidence logs. |

## Competency Verification
- Scenario-based assessments with pass threshold of 85% to retain production
  access.
- Support leads sample QA 5% of manual fallback tickets weekly.
- Compliance director confirms audit log completeness before closing training
  cycles.

## Record Keeping
- Attendance, assessment scores, and scenario notes stored in the LMS and
  mirrored to the GRC evidence locker.
- Training completion links referenced in fairness audits and regulator
  briefings.
